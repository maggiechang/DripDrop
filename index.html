Related Legislation

New Groundwater Legislation, Sustainable Groundwater Management Act

A three-bill legislative package, the Sustainable Groundwater Management Act recognizes that groundwater management in California is best accomplished locally.

California Water Code, Groundwater Management

The intent of the Groundwater Management Act is to encourage local agencies to work cooperatively to manage groundwater resources within their jurisdictions and to provide a methodology for developing a Groundwater Management Plan.

Assembly Bill 3030 (AB 3030)

AB 3030 was signed into law in 1992 and provides a systematic procedure for an existing local agency to develop a Groundwater Management Plan.

Senate Bill 1938 (SB 1938)

SB 1938 modified the Groundwater Management Act by requiring any public agency seeking State funds administered through DWR for the construction of groundwater projects to prepare and implement a Groundwater Management Plan with specified required components.

Bulleting 118 Update

The California Budget Act of 1999 authorized the update to Bulletin 118 and directed the California Department of Water Resources (DWR) to complete several tasks, including developing criteria for evaluating groundwater management plans and developing a model groundwater management ordinance.

Useful Conservation Tips

Tips for Kids

Fill up a cup of water instead of using the sink when brushing your teeth.

Listen to music while you take a shower--challenge yourself and see if you can finish your shower within 3 songs!

Instead of dumping your old drinking water in the sink, use it water your plants!

Tips for Teens/Adults

Leave grass clippings on your lawn after you mow it--this helps cool the ground and retain moisture for a longer period of time.

Put food coloring in your toilet tank--if it seeps through to the bowl within 30 minutes (without flushing), then you have a leak! Fixing it can save you A TON of water!

Do your laundry in large loads with cold water. Using cold water instead of warm water will save you 15 times the energy, which can greatly cut down your energy and water bills.

Eat one vegetarian meal a day. Replacing just 4 OUNCES of beef with vegetables a week can save more than 3,000 GALLONS of water!
